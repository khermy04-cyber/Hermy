NOUNS

A noun is a word that identifies a person,a place,a thing, or idea.

Person: a child an artist
Place: a city an island
Thing: a dog an orange
Idea: a dream an emotion

In english,we always start the names of people, places, days of the week, months of the year, and other proper nouns with a CAPITAL LETTER.

People: Mr. Smith Jennifer
Places: Paris Mount Everest
Days and Months: Monday July

Singular and Plural Nouns

Most nouns can be Singular(one) or Plural (more than one):

a pizza - two pizzas
an apple - five apples
an egg - some eggs
a melon - three melons

For the plural form of most nouns,add -(S)

bottle – bottles
pen – pens
window – windows
coin – coins

For nouns that end in (S), (SS), (ch), and (X), sounds and (potato, tomato), add (es).

box – boxes
moss – mosses
bus – buses
potato – potatoes

For nouns ending in (f), or (fe), change (f) to (v), and add (es).

wolf – wolves
wife – wives
leaf – leaves
life – lives

For nouns that end in consonant (b,c,d, etc)+ y (ay, ey, oy) change (y) to (i) and add (es and S):

day – days
baby – babies
toy – toys
family – families

The plural form of a few nouns is different.We call these Irregular nouns:

man - men
woman - women
fish - fish
tooth - teeth
foot - feet
deer - deer
child - children
mouse - mice

Nouns + verb

The bus leaves at 8:30

We use different verb forms with singular and plural nouns:

Our teacher is British.
Those teachers are Japanese.
The bus leaves at 8.30.
The buses leave from the town centre.

Some nouns are always plural:

jeans , shorts , scissors, trousers , clothes , glasses,
My jeans are blue. (Even if it's one pair of jeans)
The scissors are sharp. (Even if it's one pair of scissors)

Countable and Uncountable Nouns

Countable Nouns

Those flowers
Look Good

In english, nouns can be countable or uncountable.
Countable nouns are tgings we can count:

a burger , a car , a banana , an egg , an apple , a sandwich.

Countable nouns can be singular and often have articles like (a, an, the) or demonstratives like this/ that before them:

Where's the car?
That book is interesting.
This apple is very hard to bite.

Countable nouns also can be plural and often have articles like the or quantifiers like some or demonstratives like these/those before them:

I'd like some tomatoes, please. Those flowers look beautiful. These books are informative.
